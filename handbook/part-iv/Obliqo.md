# ![logo-obliqo.webp](/img/logo-obliqo.webp) - The Friction Engine


**Obliqo is an open experiment by Pyragogy designed to challenge the default behavior of AI writing tools. It is not an auto-complete for your thoughts; it is a cognitive friction engine that forces you to write with intention.**



---

## 1. WHY: The Case for Useful Friction

The current trajectory of Generative AI is built on a single, dominant promise: **convenience**. Most tools are designed to remove hesitation, smooth out awkward phrasing, and accelerate the journey from a blank page to a finished draft. 

While convenience is useful for drafting emails, it is detrimental to deep thinking. When AI becomes the default authorial surface, the human writer is gradually reduced to an editor of automated thought. 

* First, the system polishes.
* Then, it anticipates.
* Finally, it replaces.

We built Obliqo because we believe that **not all friction is a defect**. In the context of learning and critical writing, friction is the place where judgment is formed. A draft is not fragile just because of typos; it is fragile because it hides convenient simplifications, untested claims, and rhetorical shortcuts.

Obliqo exists to reintroduce **useful friction** into the writing process. It does not exist to make your text sound better; it exists to ask you if your argument actually holds.

---

## 2. WHAT: A Socratic Approach to AI

Obliqo is an open-source methodology and web application that treats AI not as a ghostwriter, but as a **dialectical partner**. 

Instead of generating text *for* you, Obliqo analyzes your draft and generates **Socratic resistance**. It identifies:
* **Logical leaps:** Where a conclusion does not follow from the premise.
* **Rhetorical evasion:** Overuse of adverbs or passive voice to mask uncertainty.
* **Echo chambers:** When your argument relies on unexamined biases rather than evidence.

### The "Obliqo Method"
The core of Obliqo is not its code, but its **epistemological framework**. We are building a library of "Friction Patterns"—specific prompts and logical stress-tests designed to make the writer pause and defend their work. 

The software simply executes these patterns, forcing a dialogue between the author and the text before the work can be considered "finished."

---

## 3. HOW: An Open Laboratory (and How You Can Help)

Obliqo was born within the **Pyragogy ecosystem** because we believe that the tools of thought should not be locked behind proprietary black boxes. 

The underlying philosophy, the Socratic prompts, and the Friction Patterns are being released as a **Digital Commons** (Creative Commons). The web application hosted at [obliqo.pyragogy.org](https://obliqo.pyragogy.org/) serves as our official implementation of this method.

This is a living experiment, and we need the community's intelligence to refine it. Obliqo is not a finished dogma; it is a construction site.

### Contribute to the Engine
We invite educators, writers, logicians, and technologists to help us build a better "adversary":

1.  **Define New Friction:** Are you an expert in cognitive biases or logical fallacies? Use the Discussion (Talk) section of this Wiki to propose new ways Obliqo should challenge a text.
2.  **Test the Method:** Use the Obliqo framework in your study groups, classrooms, or editorial teams. Report back on how "useful friction" changes the dynamic of peer review.
3.  **Challenge the Model:** AI often defaults to politeness. Help us engineer prompts that make the AI a rigorous, uncompromising, yet constructive sparring partner.

> *"If you want to write faster, use a standard LLM. If you want to write truer, you need friction."*
