A living handbook for human–AI co-learning.

**Pyragogy Handbook — AI Edition** is an evolving editorial project that explores what happens when AI is not treated as a tool on the side, but as a participant in the room: a cognitive presence that can support inquiry, provoke revision, surface tensions, and help stabilize new forms of shared understanding.

This repository is the **canonical source** of that handbook.

It is not a random collection of notes.  
It is not a dump of prompts.  
It is not the full backstage of the project.

It is the versioned, readable, and progressively stabilized core of an ongoing human–AI collaboration.

## Why this exists

Pyragogy begins from a simple but demanding intuition:

> learning changes when AI enters the process not only as an instrument, but as a peer in the dynamics of thought.

That shift creates new possibilities, but also new risks.  
It can accelerate reflection, but also produce noise.  
It can help build understanding, but also simulate false clarity.  
It can support co-learning, but only if the process is made explicit, disciplined, and open to revision.

This handbook exists to document that emerging space with enough structure to be useful, and enough openness to remain alive.

## How the handbook works

The handbook is built around a simple distinction:

### Backstage

The backstage is where ideas are explored, debated, drafted, challenged, and co-edited.

This is where rough material can exist:
- unfinished notes
- experiments
- tensions
- partial syntheses
- human–AI working drafts

### Frontstage

The frontstage is where selected knowledge is stabilized and published in readable form.

This repository serves that transition.

A page belongs here when it has reached a form that is coherent enough to be shared, discussed, and defended in public — even if it remains provisional.

In other words:

> what appears here is not raw generation, but the stabilized form of an ongoing cognitive process.

## Editorial and collaborative workflow

The handbook does not move directly from idea to publication.

Its working flow is intentionally layered:

### 1. Forum discussion

Proposals, revisions, doubts, and critiques are first discussed on the Pyragogy forum:

**https://forum.pyragogy.org/**

This is where contributions begin to encounter other minds.  
It is the first place where an idea is tested socially rather than merely stated.

### 2. Collaborative editing

Once a direction becomes worth developing, drafting and revision can continue collaboratively in HedgeDoc:

**https://hedgedoc.pyragogy.org/**

This stage is for live co-editing, shared rewriting, refinement, and negotiation of form.

### 3. Cognitive friction before stabilization

Before a page reaches stable publication, Pyragogy may introduce a dedicated phase of **AI-mediated cognitive friction**.

This can take the form of a brainstorm arena in which multiple AI agents examine the same page from different perspectives, generating tension, disagreement, contrast, and structural pressure before release.

The goal is not spectacle.  
It is not artificial complexity.  
It is to make weak thinking harder to stabilize.

This arena is imagined not as a hidden technical step, but as a possible public event format: a live editorial encounter, more dialogical and intellectually alive than the usual one-way presentation model.

### 4. Stable publication

Only after discussion, collaborative editing, and cognitive friction does a page become eligible for stabilization.

At that point it is:

- versioned in GitHub
- published in Wiki.js
- made part of the readable frontstage of the handbook

## Editorial philosophy

This handbook is not built as a closed doctrine.

It is built as a **structured exploration**.

That means:

- stability is editorial, not absolute
- revision is expected, not feared
- disagreement can be productive
- uncertainty is part of the method
- clarity matters more than volume
- participation matters more than performance

Pyragogy is not presented here as a finished system.  
It is an exploration that has just begun: a shared journey, not a closed and fully defined framework.

## Infrastructure in plain words

The handbook relies on a simple ecosystem:

- **Forum** for first discussion and collective reflection
- **HedgeDoc** for collaborative drafting and editing
- **Wiki.js** for the public reading surface
- **GitHub** for version history and canonical storage
- **Markdown** for portability and transparency

This combination matters.

It means the handbook can stay:
- readable in public
- editable in practice
- discussable in community
- traceable over time
- open to participation
- resilient against chaos

This may look slightly technical in the repository view, but it reflects an intentional editorial logic:
- landing pages define the role of each section
- subfolders contain the deeper pages that belong to that section
- the public wiki turns that structure into a readable handbook

## How AI fits into this project

AI is not here to replace authorship.

It is here to support a demanding form of co-thinking.

Depending on the situation, AI may help:
- surface contradictions
- propose structures
- compare versions
- preserve continuity across iterations
- challenge weak formulations
- accelerate synthesis without skipping reflection

But AI does not define truth, legitimacy, or publication status on its own.

The final responsibility for stabilization remains editorial and human.

## How to participate

Participation is welcome.

You do not need to arrive with a finished theory.  
You do not need to agree with everything.  
You do need to care about clarity, rigor, and the difference between generating text and building understanding.

A healthy contribution path looks like this:

1. open or join a discussion on the forum  
2. help sharpen the question, tension, or proposal  
3. move into collaborative drafting when the material is ready  
4. expose the page to deeper review and cognitive friction  
5. stabilize only what deserves publication

Useful contributions may include:

- improving definitions
- proposing better structures
- documenting cases and experiments
- identifying contradictions or weak points
- refining editorial language
- opening new questions worth pursuing

The best contributions do not merely add content.  
They improve the quality of thought.

## Contribution mindset

If you want to contribute, keep this in mind:

- aim for clarity over volume
- prefer useful tension over polite vagueness
- question assumptions without flattening complexity
- help stabilize what matters
- do not confuse raw output with shared understanding

This handbook grows through disciplined iteration.
